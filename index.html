Prompt 1 — AI Curriculum via Kern's 6-Step Model
You are an expert medical educator and curriculum developer specializing in radiology and AI education. Using ONLY the sources uploaded to this notebook, generate a comprehensive AI literacy curriculum for radiology residents and faculty that strictly follows Kern's 6-Step Model for Curriculum Development. Structure your output as follows: STEP 1 — PROBLEM IDENTIFICATION & GENERAL NEEDS ASSESSMENT - Define the gap between current and ideal AI literacy in radiology - Cite specific evidence from the uploaded sources STEP 2 — TARGETED NEEDS ASSESSMENT - Identify specific learning needs for: (a) junior residents, (b) senior residents, (c) attending faculty - Reference competency frameworks found in the sources STEP 3 — GOALS & OBJECTIVES - Program-level goals (3–5) - Measurable learning objectives per learner level using Bloom's Taxonomy (levels 3–5) - Map objectives to ACGME competency domains STEP 4 — EDUCATIONAL STRATEGIES - Recommended teaching formats (didactic, simulation, case-based, hands-on tool use) - Sequencing and timing of content delivery - Specific AI tools and topics to address per session STEP 5 — IMPLEMENTATION - Proposed curriculum schedule (number of sessions, duration, format) - Faculty roles and responsibilities - Required resources and infrastructure STEP 6 — EVALUATION & FEEDBACK - Formative and summative assessment methods - Metrics for measuring AI competency attainment - Feedback loops for curriculum improvement Ground every recommendation in the uploaded sources. Flag any gaps not addressed by the current literature.
Prompt 2 — AI Ethics in Radiology Slide Deck (50 min / 25 slides)
You are an expert radiology educator and AI ethicist. Using ONLY the sources uploaded to this notebook, create a detailed outline for a 50-minute lecture slide deck (exactly 25 slides) on AI Ethics in Radiology for residents and faculty. For each slide provide: slide number, title, key content points (3–5 bullets), and any recommended visuals or activities. SLIDES 1–3: INTRODUCTION & OBJECTIVES - Title slide, speaker intro, session overview - 3–4 strong measurable learning objectives (Bloom's levels 4–6) - Why AI ethics matters in radiology right now — framing the stakes SLIDES 4–8: ETHICAL FOUNDATIONS - Core ethical principles applied to AI in radiology (beneficence, non-maleficence, autonomy, justice) - Algorithmic bias and health equity — evidence from the literature - Data privacy, consent, and patient rights in AI-assisted imaging SLIDES 9–14: REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES FROM THE LITERATURE - 3 examples of GOOD ethical AI use in radiology (cite specific studies from uploaded sources) - 3 examples of BAD or problematic AI use/failures in radiology (cite specific studies from uploaded sources) - Discussion activity: small group analysis of a case scenario (5 minutes) SLIDES 15–19: REGULATORY & PROFESSIONAL FRAMEWORKS - FDA oversight of AI/ML-based software in radiology - ACR AI appropriateness criteria and ethical guidelines - Radiologist accountability when AI is wrong — medicolegal considerations SLIDES 20–23: PRACTICAL GUIDANCE FOR TRAINEES & FACULTY - How to critically evaluate an AI tool before clinical use - Documenting AI use in radiology reports - Speaking up when AI output seems wrong SLIDES 24–25: SUMMARY & CLOSING - Key takeaways (one per objective) - Resources for further learning and Q&A prompt Ground all content in the uploaded sources. Flag any slide where sources do not provide sufficient evidence so the educator can supplement.
